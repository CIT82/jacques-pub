About Me & Our Mission

Hi, I'm Cosmo, your guide through the infinite expanse of space. With a lifelong passion for astronomy and a telescope in my hands since I was a child, I created Starry Insights to share the latest news, personal insights, and the beauty of the cosmos with fellow space enthusiasts. Our mission is to make the wonders of the universe accessible to everyone, sparking curiosity and awe in our vast cosmic backyard.

Upcoming Events

Supermoon Spectacle

Event Date: March 30th, 2024

Don't miss the brightest and largest Supermoon of the year! This celestial event will light up the night sky, offering a perfect opportunity for amateur astronomers and stargazers alike. Grab your telescopes and join our live stream as we discuss the science behind the Supermoon and share tips for capturing the perfect photo.

How to Participate

Join our live webinar at 8 PM EST or head to your nearest open space with a clear view of the sky. Use the hashtag #SupermoonSpectacle to share your photos and experiences with our community!

Perseid Meteor Shower Viewing Party

Event Date: August 12-13th, 2024

The Perseid meteor shower is making its annual appearance, promising a dazzling display of shooting stars. With up to 100 meteors per hour, this is an event you won't want to miss. We're hosting a virtual viewing party and a guide on how to observe this beautiful spectacle from the comfort of your backyard.

How to Participate

Find a dark spot away from city lights on the night of August 12th, lie back, and watch the show. Tune into our live commentary at 11 PM EST for expert insights and interaction with fellow enthusiasts.

Venus and Jupiter Conjunction

Event Date: November 2nd, 2024

Witness a breathtaking conjunction of Venus and Jupiter as they come visibly close in the early morning sky, appearing as bright celestial neighbors. This rare event is a beautiful display of planetary motion. We'll explain the astronomical significance and how to best view the conjunction.

How to Participate

Wake up early and look to the eastern horizon about 45 minutes before sunrise. No special equipment needed, though binoculars can enhance your viewing experience. Share your observations and pictures with us on social media using #VenusJupiterMeet.
